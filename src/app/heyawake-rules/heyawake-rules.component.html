1. The numbers in some outlined areas indicate how many squares are painted; if there is no number, there may be any number of painted squares, including zero
  2. Painted squares cannot touch each other vertically or horizontally, but they can be diagonal
  3. All white squares form a contiguous area, forming a path that can get to each other white square by a series of horizontal or vertical steps (diagonally does not count)
  4. A line of white squares, running either horizontally or vertically, may not span more than two outlined areas
